What is it?

Embodied Making is a sensemaking and problem-solving method for complex situations. It uses stories of the people in those situations to understand their experiences. Forces, or the reasons those situations occur, are derived from stories or directly from experience. The forces are balanced and resolved by solutions. The entirety of solutions are inter-purposed through patterns.

Who can use it?

Embodied Making can be used by everyone. We all read and tell stories. We constantly have to resolve the forces created by others and by ourselves. We all live with the solutions of modern society around us, ranging from public transport systems to coffee machines. We all need to sense of the complexity around us, and be sure that our choices are balancing the forces around us.

When to use it?

You are probably using the elements of Embodied Making at some level in making decisions in your everyday life. You can use it for relatively simple tasks like choosing the destination of your next holiday, furnishing an office, or arranging a shop. And you can use it for relatively complex tasks like building software systems, urban planning, or structuring telecommunications infrastructure.

Introduction to the method

Embodied Making isn’t understood in a few steps but has to be embodied by the entity who applies it.

1. Problems

You probably encounter any number of problems everyday. Some are easy to solve. Others are more complex.

2. The right solutions?

How can you be sure that the solutions you select will solve these problems and not make them worse?

3. Problem > Solution?

Most problem-solving methods work in steps. Understand the problem. Think about the problem. Think of solutions.

4. Complete complexity

By separating thinking and doing, solutions are made without understanding or experiencing the complete complexity.

5. Thinking AND doing

Embodied Making is thinking and doing simultaneously, making sense of complexity through action and reflection.

6. Understand experiences

Making sense of an environment requires understanding the unique experiences of different individuals in that environment.

7. Understand with stories

The most reliable method of understanding experiences is with stories of unique individuals, avoiding archetypal anecdotes.

8. Unique experiences

Well-told stories help understand the unique experiences of others almost as if we’re experiencing it ourselves.

9. Uncover forces

Stories of individual experiences uncover forces, which are any reason many individuals have similar experiences.

10. Rationalize forces

Some forces can be rationalized, and others like emotions, which also motivate choices, are harder to rationalize.

11. Treat forces equally

Forces become less or more important in different situations. Forces are treated equally to prevent bias for some situations.

12. Don't ignore or prioritize

Understanding how many forces act on each other can be difficult, but Embodied Making does not ignore or prioritize forces.

13. We do it all the time

We are able to resolve the complexity of several forces simultaneously when raising children or walking in a busy city.

14. Feel and balance

Embodied Making is a contemplative method to feel and balance the effect of several forces simultaneously.

15. Improving solutions

By feeling how forces act together, solutions are progressively improved by balancing trade-offs between forces.

16. Solutions

Solutions can resolve (+), conflict (-), or neither resolve nor conflict (~) with forces.

17. Frameworks

Frameworks can be applied to structure solutions. Applied too early, they limit the scale of insights and serendipity.

18. Refine

Frameworks can be used to refine solutions, and good solutions can be used to refine frameworks.

19. Solutions co-exist

It isn’t usually a single solution that solves complex situations, but several solutions that co-exist with each other.

20. Solution structure

Solutions must be self-sustaining while complementing other solutions, creating an inter-purposed solution structure.

21. Patterns

Patterns are reusable solutions that can selectively applied to create many solution variations and yet have a common quality.

22. Solutions for everything

Embodied Making can be used to make almost any solution, such as a computer system, building layout, or city plan.

23. Solutions can grow

By recursively using stories, forces, and solutions, practitioners can build adaptive solutions that grow as they learn.

24. Start using it

The best way to learn about Embodied Making is by using it with an embodied attitude.
